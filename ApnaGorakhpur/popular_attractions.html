Introduction

Gorakhpur is located in the eastern part of Uttar Pradesh, on the banks of the Rapti River. The city is surrounded by the districts of Deoria, Kushinagar, Siddharthnagar, and Maharajganj.

Gorakhpur is well connected with all major cities of India.

History of the temple

Gorakhnath Temple was founded in the 12th century by Gorakhnath himself. The temple is said to have been built on the site of a hermitage where Gorakhnath used to meditate. The temple has been destroyed and rebuilt several times over the centuries, but it has always remained an important pilgrimage site for yogis and Hindus.

The Rituals and Festivals

Gorakhnath Temple is a vibrant place of worship, and it is home to a number of important rituals and festivals. The most important festival at the temple is the Maha Shivratri festival, which is celebrated in February or March. During this festival, thousands of devotees visit the temple to worship Shiva, the god of destruction and regeneration.

The Akhand Jyoti and Dhuna

The Gorakhnath Temple is a sacred place for the Nath Yogis. It is said that the unbroken light (Akhand Jyoti) and smoke (Dhuna) that is present in the temple was brought by Guru Gorakhnath himself in the Treta Yug.

The temple has been attacked and destroyed several times over the centuries, but the Akhand Jyoti and Dhuna have always remained safe.
