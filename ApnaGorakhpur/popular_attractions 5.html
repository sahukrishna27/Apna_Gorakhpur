Geeta Vatika

Introduction

Geeta Vatika is a beautiful garden located in Gorakhpur, India. The garden is dedicated to the Bhagavad Gita, one of the most important texts in Hinduism. The garden is home to a number of temples, shrines, and other religious structures. It is also a popular tourist destination, known for its serene atmosphere and its beautiful landscaping.

History

Geeta Vatika was founded in 1965 by Mahant Avaidyanath, the then-head of the Gorakhnath Matth. The matth is a Hindu monastery that has been in existence for over 1,000 years. Mahant Avaidyanath was a scholar of the Bhagavad Gita, and he wanted to create a garden that would be a place for people to learn about the text and to meditate on its teachings.

The Garden

The garden is spread over 100 acres of land. It is divided into several sections, each of which is dedicated to a different aspect of the Bhagavad Gita.

The Landscape

The garden is beautifully landscaped. It is home to a variety of trees, flowers, and plants. There are also a number of fountains and water features in the garden. The overall effect is one of peace and tranquility.
