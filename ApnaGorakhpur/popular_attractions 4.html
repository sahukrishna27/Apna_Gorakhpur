Introduction

Gorakhpur Zoo, also known as Shaheed Ashfaq Ullah Khan Prani Udyan, is a zoo located in Gorakhpur, Uttar Pradesh, India. The zoo is spread over an area of 121.34 acres and is home to over 1,000 animals, including lions, tigers, elephants, rhinos, giraffes, and a variety of birds. The zoo was established in 2021 and is named after Ashfaqulla Khan, an Indian independence activist who was born in Gorakhpur. The zoo is a popular tourist destination and is a great place to learn about the wildlife of India.

History

The Gorakhpur Zoo was established in 1989. The zoo was initially set up as a rescue center for animals that were injured or orphaned. However, the zoo soon grew in popularity and became a major tourist attraction
